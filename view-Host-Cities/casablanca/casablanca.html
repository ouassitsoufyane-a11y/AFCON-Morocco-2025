About

Located in the Maârif district of Casablanca, Mohammed V Stadium stands as one of the monuments of African football. Since its inauguration on 6 March 1955, this emblematic arena - nicknamed “Donor” by locals - has hosted some of the greatest Moroccan and continental football events. With a current capacity of 67 000, the stadium is ready to welcome eight matches of the TotalEnergies CAF Africa Cup of Nations Morocco 2025.

A historical and architectural legacy

Designed by French architect Achille Dangleterre, the stadium was first named Marcel-Cerdan Stadium after the famous French-Moroccan boxer, then became the Stadium of Honour, before adopting its current name, Mohammed V, during the 1983 Mediterranean Games. Over the decades, it has undergone numerous renovations (1983, 2000, 2016, 2019, and 2025) to modernize its facilities, enhance spectator comfort, and meet FIFA and CAF international standards. Today, the Mohammed V complex extends beyond the stadium itself: it includes a 15,000-seat indoor arena, a hotel, an ulympic indoor swimming poul, a 650 m² media centre, and conference, medical, and anti-doping facilities - making it a complete and modern sports centre.

A shrine for Morocco’s greatest clubs
